Repair Shop

Shop Knowledge Network

Participating shops get smarter part suggestions from anonymized patterns across the network — your team still confirms every line before it hits a job.

Faster suggestions, human review

On work orders with equipment linked, most suggested part lines come from your shop's own history first, then similar make/model/engine units, then anonymized patterns from other participating shops. Nothing is added automatically — techs and writers confirm or reject each suggestion.

What is shared

  • Equipment make, model, and engine type (for example Freightliner, Cascadia, Detroit DD15)
  • Anonymized part descriptions and part numbers on matching equipment
  • Aggregated counts that help the network catalog reflect common equipment in the field

What is never shared

  • Customer names, contact information, or billing details
  • Unit numbers, VINs, plates, or driver-identifying information
  • Work order numbers, invoices, pricing, labor rates, or shop financials
  • Suggestions from partner shops without staff review and confirmation

Patterns only appear in licensed commercial exports when enough independent shops contribute to meet anonymity thresholds — protecting any single shop from being identified.

Commercial licensing

In-app knowledge sharing is part of the RepairFront platform experience and is governed by the EULA. OEMs, dealer groups, and analytics partners may license anonymized aggregates separately under a data agreement.
